The Studio

DHYAI is a material and surface design practice based in Bengaluru. It works across objects, textiles, and material studies for homes, interiors, private events, gifting, and hospitality.

The name ध्यै is Sanskrit — to fix the mind upon.
A fold. A weight. A softened edge. A repeated touch.
Some objects mark a moment. Others remain after the moment has passed. Dhyai works with the latter.

Each piece begins with material. Weight, texture, density, and the way a surface interacts with light are treated as primary conditions. Form is derived from these conditions. The work begins with how an object holds: through handling, wear, and continued presence within a space. Objects are developed for use, handling, and retention.

The studio focuses on the intersection of technical product engineering, architectural structure, surface rhythm, and tactile permanence.

The work is produced in small numbers, by hand, in workshops the studio has chosen to work with over time.
Nothing is hurried.

Textile

DHYAI works with all textiles: pure cotton, muslin, silk and more. The fabrics are chosen for how they drape, how they hold shadows, and how they soften with lived experience.

Textile object construction and design extend multiple techniques, both modern and traditional. Be it hand block print, kantha stitch, age old madhubani painting or modern digital prints rooted in conceptual design language.

The current studies are not surface decoration. They are object families — bedsheets, bedcovers, dohars, table covers, table linen sets — developed through repetition, spacing, weight, and use.

Each design is born out of reason and experiential phenomenon. It is a memory. It is a moment. It is a monument.

Cast Brass

Intimate intellectual and spiritual experiences bring out the conception of objects that are then translated into the grammar of material. Material is only a medium. And material such as brass, embodies that grammar most generously.

The brass develops a living patina over time, where the surface deepens with use. And the weight of the material is essential to its presence in a room.

Brass pieces are sand-cast, lathe-finished, and satin-brushed.
