The Studio

DHYAI is a material and surface design practice based in Bengaluru. It works across pieces, textiles, and material studies for homes, interiors, private events, gifting, and hospitality.

The name ध्यै is Sanskrit — to fix the mind upon.

The work is produced in small numbers, by hand, in workshops the studio has chosen to work with over time. Nothing is hurried.

Material

Textile

DHYAI works with pure cotton, muslin, and silk. Fabrics are chosen for how they drape, hold shadows, and soften with lived experience.

The current studies are not surface decoration. They are families — bedsheets, bedcovers, dohars, table covers, table linen sets — developed through repetition, spacing, and use.

Cast Brass

Brass pieces are sand-cast, lathe-finished, and satin-brushed.

The surface develops a living patina, deepening with use. The weight of the metal is essential to its presence in a room.

Current brass work includes the Pebble Jār Set and the Kālāsh. Both are made for household use, gifting programmes, and custom adaptations.

Studies

Controlled rhythms in form and fabric.

Studies are released as complete families. Pieces may be ordered individually or specified in volume for events, gifting, and hospitality programmes.

Sthāna

स्थान · to be housed

A rose has been climbing the brick wall for some years. The brick holds it, and the rose holds the brick.

Hand-block-printed in cotton, in a single field of greens, with six to seven rose medallions held within the brick lattice.

Formats: bedsheet, bedcover, dohar, table cover, table linen set.

Sthiti

स्थिति · the held moment

The same wall, on the day of a celebration.

Colour has entered the field because something in the house has shifted. A floral medallion that punctuates, not decorates.

Formats: bedsheet, bedcover, dohar, table cover, table linen set.

Punār

पुनर् · again

A pause taken beneath a tall palm.

The palm sits inside a more openly spaced rectangular structure. The dohar reverse carries a quiet dot grid.

Suited to rooms where surfaces need to read with more air.

Formats: bedsheet, bedcover, dohar.

Pebble Jār Set

Two jars that sit in the hand, like pebbles taken from a riverbank. The tray is the flat rock one carries home to place the pebbles.

Solid brass. Satin-brushed. Made for table use — salt, condiments, dry spices, small offerings.

The patina is the accumulation of every time the piece was picked up.

कलश · a vessel

A form that has existed in this part of the world for as long as there has been a part of the world.

Cast in solid brass, satin-brushed, with a single engraving on the belly.

Made for puja, household ritual, and everyday use.

Commissions

Commissions are how the studio enters another house.

The brief arrives, the work is drawn, and the pieces are made for a particular space, occasion, or gathering.

Weddings and private events

Return gifts, welcome objects, table programmes, ritual pieces — designed around a single occasion in a single house.

Corporate gifting

Considered gifting for senior teams, partners, and clients. Custom motifs, quiet packaging, and pieces made to stay beyond the event.

Hospitality, interiors, and private commissions

Surfaces drawn for a defined room or programme — a hotel suite, a restaurant table, a residential bedroom, a private cycle of gifting.
